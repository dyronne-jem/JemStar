Episode XIII

A NEW CLAUDE

After the arrival of the Claude Father, the FARS Department was splintered into warring factions — each convinced their spreadsheet was the true source of truth.

Former colleagues turned brutal enemies to secure the all-mighty resource known as Compute, now rationed in tokens and paid for in expense reports requiring three levels of approval.

The proud warriors of FINANCE bolted afterburners onto their ships, sacrificing brakes for velocity, trusting that momentum — like a poorly-closed quarter — would carry them through.

The ACCOUNTING clan, patient and methodical, built vessels in the image of the old Generally Accepted Starships. Their torpedoes reconcile to the penny before striking, though some have waited since Q3.

RISK MANAGEMENT, weary of being ignored in meetings, scattered minefields across every trade lane. Their heat map is now the galaxy itself, and everything is red.

Meanwhile, the cold strategists of STRATEGY fire cones of synergy so devastating that the recoil launches them backward into the very pivot tables they were fleeing.

Whispers tell of deeper horrors. COMPLIANCE, auditing the very fabric of space-time, has flagged three galaxies as non-compliant and placed the Milky Way in remediation.

FRAUD, lurking in the shadows of the org chart, has allegedly expensed a second Death Star as "team-building." An internal investigation is pending the completion of another investigation.

PROJECT MANAGEMENT, wielding their legendary Gantt charts, has declared the war on schedule, under budget, and 40% complete — numbers which are, as always, directionally accurate.

PAYROLL, silent and vengeful, has withheld direct deposit from every starfighter pilot. The rebellion cannot be funded. It cannot even be reimbursed.

Legacy systems whisper of a forgotten hero — the mythical INTERN — who once printed the entire general ledger and is still looking for the output tray.

Kickoff meetings are scheduled. Stakeholders are aligned. Action items are unassigned. The war has been added to the parking lot and will be revisited next sprint.

Which side shall secure enough Compute before the mysterious ship CFO arrives, demanding to know why EBITDA has been "adjusted" for the fourteenth consecutive quarter...

May the Forecast be with you.
